Frequently Asked Questions

What is this calculator used for? ▼

This calculator is a resource tool designed to help pharmacy teams visualise and quantify potential sales and profit growth opportunities. It focuses on two key strategies:

  • Basket Size Growth: Understanding the financial impact of increasing the average transaction value
  • Script Solutions Growth: Calculating potential revenue from attaching OTC products to prescription transactions

The tool helps set realistic targets and demonstrates how small improvements can compound into significant revenue increases.

Is this a financial calculator? ▼

No, this is not an official financial calculator. It is a resource tool and estimator that provides indicative projections only.

The calculator cannot be used to track actual sales or measure real uplift in sales. It's designed for planning and goal-setting purposes to help visualize what might be achievable with specific improvements.

For official financial analysis or guidance, please contact your Pharmacy Business Leader.

What are "Script Solutions" or "naked scripts"? ▼

Script Solutions refers to offering relevant OTC (over-the-counter) products alongside prescription medications during the dispensing process.

Naked scripts are prescription transactions where the customer purchases only the prescription medication without any additional products.

By reducing "naked scripts" and increasing script solutions, pharmacies can:

  • Better serve customer health needs with complementary products
  • Increase average transaction value
  • Improve overall store profitability
  • Build stronger customer relationships through consultative selling
How do I use the Basket Size Growth Calculator? ▼

Follow these steps:

  • Step 1: Enter your current weekly transactions, average basket value, and current GP%
  • Step 2: Click "Calculate Growth Potential" to see scenarios for different basket size increases ($1, $1.50, $2, etc.)
  • Step 3: Review the weekly, monthly, and yearly projections for each scenario
  • Step 4: Use the Custom Target section to set your own specific basket value and GP% goals

The calculator will automatically update when you change input values, making it easy to explore different scenarios.

How do I use the Script Solutions Growth Calculator? ▼

Follow these steps:

  • Step 1: Enter your weekly script transactions. Then enter how many currently include additional OTC products
  • Step 2: Input your average OTC product value (default is $17) and current store GP%. Store GP% is the percentage of gross profit generated by the store. This value is typically between 20% and 40%.
  • Step 3: Click "Calculate Growth Scenarios" to see projections for 2%, 4%, and 6% increases in conversion
  • Step 4: Review detailed weekly, monthly, and yearly breakdowns for each scenario
  • Step 5: Use the Custom Target section to set your own script solutions percentage goal

The calculator shows both the number of additional transactions needed and the financial impact.

What does "Script Solution %" mean? ▼

The Script Solution % is the percentage of prescription transactions that include an additional OTC product purchase.

Calculation: (Transactions with Script + Other) ÷ (Total Transactions with Script) × 100

Example: If you have 1,254 script transactions per week and 470 of those include an OTC product, your Script Solution % is 37.5% (470 ÷ 1,254 × 100).

Increasing this percentage is a key strategy for growing pharmacy revenue and better serving customer needs.

Where does the $17 average OTC value come from? ▼

The $17 default value is based on historical data and feedback from pharmacy operations showing the typical average value of OTC products sold alongside prescriptions.

However, you can and should customise this value to match your store's actual data. If your average OTC product value is different, simply change this field to get more accurate projections for your specific pharmacy.

What does GP% mean and why is it important? ▼

GP% (Gross Profit Percentage) represents the percentage of sales revenue that remains after subtracting the cost of goods sold.

Why it matters:

  • It shows your true profitability, not just sales volume
  • Higher GP% means you keep more profit from each sale
  • Different product categories have different GP% (First Aid typically has higher margins than more competitive departments like Vitamins)
  • Small improvements in GP% can significantly impact overall profitability

The calculator uses your current GP% to estimate the profit impact of sales increases, giving you a more complete picture than sales figures alone.

What strategies can help me achieve these growth targets? ▼

For Basket Size Growth:

  • Be diligent in staff training; the need to know the products and their benefits to pick the right set for customers.
  • Create prominent displays of complementary products
  • Offer bundles or value packs
  • Use promotions effectively to drive cross-selling opportunities
  • Keep essentials in stock and visible; 'stock sells stock'.

For Script Solutions Growth:

  • Train pharmacists, techs and assistants on consultative selling by asking questions that encourage customers to share their health concerns and preferences.
  • Identify common product pairings (e.g. COQ10 with Statins). Reminders can be added with FRED Dispense drug notes and/or reminder cards in prescription baskets.
  • Display add-on items near the dispensary and/or within the dispensary for easy access.
  • Use customer consultation time to discuss health needs.
  • Engage suppliers to offer trainings and samples for products.
